Game of Thrones is an extremely popular television show based off books by author George R. R. Martin. It took the entire world by storm when it aired in April 2011 and would continue to thrive through the decade up to its finale which aired on May 2019.

Season 1 averaged 9.3 million viewers, and by season 8 the show was pulling in an average of 44.2 million viewers. Game of Thrones managed to win 59 Emmy awards and broke records including being the most expensive television show made averaging $15 million per episode in Season 8.

So why was Game of Thrones so popular? The first season did have a $60 million budget, but you can't just throw money at something and expect it to be good (Cats the movie for example). Having a multitude of fantastic actors and actresses probably helped as well.

But ultimately, the books that the show was based on were already well known by 2011 having been on New York Times Best Seller List. And for good reason too. George R. R. Martin had created a well-crafted and complex world with a vast array of characters each having their own separate story.

This all sounds good and everything, right? Wrong. There is one ultimate flaw in this. This flaw would lead Game of Thrones to have its worst rated season, season 8, which has a rating of 58% (critic) on RottenTomatoes, the only season to rate below 90%. The flaw is that George R. R. Martin has not finished the novels yet.

In fact, he still has two planned and unreleased novels in the works. So without having source material to go off of, the showrunners had to try their best to tie in all the storylines together. And we saw how they went. (We're all at home, and no one is talking about rewatching Game of Thrones.)
